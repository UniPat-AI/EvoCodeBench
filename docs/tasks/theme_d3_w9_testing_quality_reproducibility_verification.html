Difficulty analysis

This task implements repro-verify from a written spec (/app/docs/spec_v1.md): a CLI that verifies computational reproducibility — running deterministic checks, detecting nondeterminism, normalizing environments, matching against a reference, and producing summary/diff/graph reports over a manifest of steps. 7 rounds across 12 models. The spec-driven nature means the exact command surface and JSON output shapes are fixed, and the round verifier is all-or-nothing.

The commonly-failed requirements span the core verification logic and its reporting: nondeterminism-detect and reference-match trip 10 of 12 models; verify-deterministic, env-normalization, cross-pass-consistency and report-summary each catch 9 of 12. Failure reasons cluster into two modes — wrong exit codes and wrong boolean output. nondeterminism-detect exits 2 instead of the expected code; reference-match returns "got false" where true was required; env-normalization reports "status:ERROR"; and cross-pass-consistency returns "got true" unexpectedly. report-summary consistently exposes an accounting error: models emit "passed=0" when the verifier expects a nonzero count.

Performance gap

The divide across 12 models is stark. Opus-4.8-xhigh and MiniMax-M3 are the only solvers: both clear 7/7 rounds at 100% case pass. Every other model scores 0/7 — a clean binary split between two perfect runs and ten complete failures at the round level.

Case pass reveals the spread within those 10 zeros. Qwen3.7-Max reaches 90% case (0/7), followed by Qwen3.6-Plus at 80% and GPT-5.5 at 77% — models that implement much of the logic but trip the exit-code and boolean-output requirements (nondeterminism-detect, reference-match, cross-pass-consistency) that gate every round. Kimi-K2.7-Code sits at 71%, GLM-5.2 at 55%, MiniMax-M2.7 at 35%. The bottom tier collapses genuinely: Kimi-K2.6 22%, GLM-5.1 18%, DeepSeek-V4-Pro 17%, and DeepSeek-V4-Flash at 13% barely executes. The headline lesson is that spec-exact output contracts — exit codes and boolean fields — create a hard wall that 90%-case models hit just as firmly as models scoring under 20%.
