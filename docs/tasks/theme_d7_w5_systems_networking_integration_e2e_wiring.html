Difficulty analysis

This task builds a Go service-mesh health router from scratch: three integrated components (health monitor, traffic router, rate limiter) wired over a Unix-socket IPC, accumulating to 45 → 147 cumulative test cases across the 8 rounds. Because each round's verifier checks every still-active requirement and the round reward is all-or-nothing, missing even 2 of 45 cases scores the round 0. No model passes a single round cleanly, so the round table is a wall of zeros — but the case-level view shows that is a scoring artifact, not uniform failure.

The genuinely hard sub-features, failed by 6–7 of the 10 models, are: circuit-breaker (the half-open → closed state machine — reasons like "State: half_open"), health-monitoring (endpoint health transitions — "Got  healthy"), drain (graceful connection draining), weight-zero (a healthy endpoint with weight 0 must receive no traffic), ipc-get-service (per-endpoint circuit state over IPC — "Got 0"), and retry / rate-limit-burst ("Only 0 succeeded out of 8"). These are stateful, timing-sensitive behaviours — exactly what tends to break when an agent implements the happy path but not the transitions.

Performance gap

Two tiers emerge. Opus-4.8 and GPT-5.5 complete all 8 rounds (72% and 82% case pass) — they build the whole system and only lose specific features. Their weaknesses differ: GPT-5.5's misses concentrate in ipc-access-log and ipc-access-stats (access-logging over IPC, 24+20 cases), while Opus loses more on health-monitoring, warmup and connection-limit-mode.

The other eight models abort the chain early — Kimi after round 3, the rest after round 1–2, and DeepSeek-V4-Flash / MiniMax-M2.7 never produce a passing round at all. This is the shared-sandbox fragility of a networking task (leftover bound ports / background processes from one round's tests poisoning the next) interacting with weaker implementations. Among the early-aborters, Qwen3.6-Plus and GLM-5.1 are also genuinely weaker on the cases they do reach (46% / 51%), whereas Qwen3.7-Max and DeepSeek-V4-Pro pass ~81% before stopping — high case quality but unable to keep the workspace stable across rounds.
