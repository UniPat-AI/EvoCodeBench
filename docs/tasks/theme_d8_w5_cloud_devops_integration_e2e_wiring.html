Difficulty analysis

This task builds multiservice-deploy-pipeline, a single bash CLI at /app/pipeline.sh that grows over 11 rounds into a full deploy orchestrator: build, topological ordering, deploy and promote across environments, diff, rollback and restore, scale, health and quota management, config import/export. Each round adds requirements to the same script and the cumulative suite reaches 638 cases — so one regression anywhere zeros the round.

The commonly-failed requirements — each missed by all 10 models — are rollback.basic, rollback.restore-previous, history.event-log, config.import, scale.get and scale.all, joined by diff.compare and promote.conflict across most models. The reasons are subtle output-contract mismatches rather than missing features — fine formatting and edge-case details that fail an all-or-nothing round even when the pipeline basically works.

Performance gap

Round scores are near-uniformly low — only Opus-4.8 and Qwen3.7-Max reach 1/11, everyone else 0/11 — so case pass tells the real story, and it ranges enormously. Opus-4.8 (96% cases), Kimi-K2.6 (94%) and DeepSeek-V4-Pro (92%) clearly build the whole orchestrator and lose only narrow output-contract edges (Kimi: diff.compare, rollback.basic, scale). These models pass the overwhelming majority of cases yet score 0–1 rounds — the binary reward hiding nearly-finished pipelines.

The weak tier is genuinely broken at the case level. GPT-5.5 collapses to 9% case pass — its diff.compare alone fails 180 cases, with order.toposort (153) and promote.conflict (149) close behind — far below its usual tier, suggesting an early structural mistake that poisoned the whole chain. GLM-5.1 sits at 34%, with MiniMax-M3 (76%), DeepSeek-V4-Flash (70%), MiniMax-M2.7 (66%) and Qwen3.6-Plus (68%) in between, each with a different broken subsystem. So this task separates models almost entirely at the case level: the round score says everyone failed, but case pass spans 9% to 96%.
