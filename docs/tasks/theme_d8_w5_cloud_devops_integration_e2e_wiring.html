Difficulty analysis

This task builds multiservice-deploy-pipeline, a single bash CLI at /app/pipeline.sh that grows over 11 rounds into a full deploy orchestrator: build, topological ordering, deploy and promote across environments, diff, rollback and restore, scale, health and quota management, config import/export. Each round adds requirements to the same script and the cumulative suite reaches hundreds of cases — so one regression anywhere zeros the round.

The commonly-failed requirements — each missed by all 12 models — are scale.get, scale.all, scale.set, health.set-get, health.filter and health.scale-interaction. The reasons are subtle output-contract mismatches rather than missing features — fine formatting and edge-case details that fail an all-or-nothing round even when the pipeline basically works.

Performance gap

Round scores are near-uniformly low across 12 models — Opus-4.8 stands alone at 5/11, Qwen3.6-Plus reaches 2/11, GPT-5.5 1/11, and all nine remaining models score 0/11 — so case pass tells the real story, and it ranges from 9% to 98%. Opus-4.8 (98% cases) clearly builds the whole orchestrator and loses only narrow output-contract edges. Qwen3.6-Plus (82%) and GPT-5.5 (79%) form a second tier, with Kimi-K2.7-Code (71%) and DeepSeek-V4-Flash (70%) close behind.

The weak tier is genuinely broken at the case level. MiniMax-M2.7 collapses to 9% case pass, with GLM-5.1, DeepSeek-V4-Pro and MiniMax-M3 all at 21%, and GLM-5.2 at 38% — far below the field, suggesting early structural mistakes that poisoned the whole chain. The universally-failing requirements scale.get, scale.set, health.filter and health.scale-interaction reveal that even the stronger models cannot satisfy the precise output contracts required for health and scaling subsystems to interact correctly.
