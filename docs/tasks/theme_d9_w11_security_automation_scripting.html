Difficulty analysis

This task builds secsentinel, a security-log analysis and automated threat-response CLI in Go: it ingests structured security events and computes timelines, severity histograms, burst/escalation scores, threat snapshots, deduplicated alerts, actor aggregation, risk prioritization and OLS forecasting — 15 rounds, the longest analytics chain here. The work is numeric and statistical, so correctness is exact: many tests compare floating-point outputs to tight tolerances ("expected=0.0625 … tol=0.001").

The hardest requirements are the statistical sub-commands. timeline and its variants (severity-histogram, burst-score, escalation-index, risk-trend) fail for several models with empty output — "expected=150.0 actual=" — meaning the metric was never computed. prioritize.score (the risk-scoring formula), forecast.ols (ordinary-least-squares prediction), histogram bucketing/percentiles and snapshot.campaigns are the other heavily-missed items. Even the strongest of the 12 models consistently brush against the malformed-input handlers — summarize.malformed, respond.malformed, diff.errors and analyze.malformed — which surface off-by-one or missing-output failures that zero out an otherwise-passing round.

Performance gap

This task discriminates sharply at the round level. GPT-5.5 and DeepSeek-V4-Pro each reach 14/15 at 100% cases, missing only one round apiece, and Qwen3.7-Max follows at 13/15 at 100% cases — these three implemented essentially the entire statistical toolkit correctly. A clear second tier sits at 9–11 rounds: Qwen3.6-Plus 11/15 (95%), DeepSeek-V4-Flash 9/15 (96%), Kimi-K2.6 10/15 (99%), and Opus-4.8 and MiniMax-M3 both 8/15 (96%).

Below that, round scores collapse even where case coverage stays moderate: Kimi-K2.7-Code and GLM-5.1 each manage only 4/15 (72% and 81% cases), while MiniMax-M2.7 and GLM-5.2 clear zero rounds (87% and 37% cases respectively). The MiniMax-M2.7 result is the binary-reward signature in miniature — 87% of cases pass but not a single round clears, because each round bundles a formula (prioritize.score, summarize.malformed, diff.errors) that never quite reaches the required precision. GLM-5.2 likewise scores 0/15 rounds at only 37% cases, the weakest case coverage in the field. The universal bottleneck across 12 models is the four malformed-input requirements: summarize.malformed (11 models fail), respond.malformed and diff.errors (10 models each), and analyze.malformed (9 models), plus the numeric prioritize.score tolerance (6 models).
