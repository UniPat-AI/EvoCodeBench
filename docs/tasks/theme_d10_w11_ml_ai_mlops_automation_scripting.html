Difficulty analysis

This task builds mlpipe, a Go CLI for orchestrating ML experiment pipelines: plan a DAG, run stages, sweep hyperparameters, compare and report results, and export to JSON/CSV — 7 rounds, with an all-or-nothing round reward across every active requirement. A single failing contract zeros the round even when nearly everything else passes, making each incremental extension a compounding hazard across 12 models.

The load-bearing failures cluster in statistical and validation plumbing layered on top of the core orchestrator. compare-spearman and export-filter each trip 8 models, while compare-tie, run-params, report-std, and compare-pareto each catch 7 — details such as Spearman rho with ties, Bessel-corrected standard deviation, Pareto-frontier counting, and filter exit codes that demand precise numeric and behavioral contracts rather than structural correctness alone.

Performance gap

Opus-4.8-xhigh (4/7, 100% case) and Kimi-K2.7-Code (3/7, 100% case) are the only models to pass any rounds, followed distantly by GPT-5.5 at 1/7 (99% case) and Kimi-K2.6 at 1/7 (60% case). These top models implement the orchestrator core correctly and lose rounds only to edge contracts — compare-spearman, compare-pareto, report-std, and run-params are the requirements that block them from advancing further.

The remaining 8 models score 0/7. Qwen3.7-Max (99% case) and MiniMax-M2.7 (75%) come closest in raw coverage but cannot clear the statistical precision hurdles. DeepSeek-V4-Pro (68%), Qwen3.6-Plus (64%), and MiniMax-M3 (48%) represent a mid tier that gets through basic pipeline logic but misses multiple compare and export contracts, while DeepSeek-V4-Flash (27%), GLM-5.2 (16%), and GLM-5.1 (11%) indicate failures deeper in the orchestrator — likely at the planning or run-execution layer before compare and export are even reached.
