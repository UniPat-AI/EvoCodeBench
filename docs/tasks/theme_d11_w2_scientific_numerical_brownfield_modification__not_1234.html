Difficulty analysis

This task implements nlsolve, a sparse-matrix iterative solver toolkit in C: read a linear system Ax = b from files, solve it with CG/GMRES plus preconditioners, analyze matrix structure, and report convergence — 5 rounds. Writing this in C (manual memory, exact stdout/stderr discipline) raises the bar on the interface details rather than the math.

Two requirements defeat nearly the whole field. error-handling (failed by 6 models) demands exact failure behaviour — e.g. a non-square matrix must "exit 1, single error: line on stderr, empty stdout" — and generators (6 models) requires the synthetic-matrix builders to produce "correct dimensions, NNZ, entries, and silent stdout", where models leak extra output or get a Laplacian diagonal entry wrong. Also commonly missed: duplicate-entry-merge and gmres-solver (6 each), plus true-residual and preconditioners (5 each) — all precise output/convergence contracts.

Performance gap

The remarkable thing is how flat the round scores are against how high the case scores run. GLM-5.1 leads with just 2/5 rounds — at 99% case pass — losing only a handful of gmres-solver (4), duplicate-entry-merge (3) and rcm-disconnected (2) cases. Opus-4.8 also sits at 99% case pass but scores only 1/5, undone almost entirely by error-handling (16 cases) — a near-perfect solver penalized for not matching the exact error-output contract.

Everyone else is 0/5 rounds, yet most are nearly complete: GPT-5.5 and Kimi-K2.6 at 96% each miss only generators (35), DeepSeek-V4-Flash at 97% loses scattered gmres-solver and preconditioners cases, and MiniMax-M3 (94%) and DeepSeek-V4-Pro (93%) are close behind. Only the bottom two genuinely struggle — Qwen3.7-Max at 60% and MiniMax-M2.7 at 23%, the latter dumping 266 cases into error-handling and 80 into gmres-solver. So eight models are functionally within a few percent of each other on cases, but the binary round reward — gated on the unforgiving error-handling and generators contracts — hands them all a zero.
