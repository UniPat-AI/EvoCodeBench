Difficulty analysis

This is a brownfield build-automation task: extend forgekit, a bash-based build orchestrator for a monorepo of eight C packages (four libraries, four services), so it does dependency-aware incremental builds — content-hash caching, mtime tracking, selective rebuilds, impact analysis, and profile-scoped clean — across 8 rounds. The agent must reproduce subtle filesystem and caching semantics in shell.

Two requirements dominate everything across all 12 models: cache-manifest-mtime (11/12, "manifest touch should update libbase artifact mtime") and content-hash-cache (10/12, "Makefile content change must bump the downstream artifact mtime") — both require propagating mtime through the dependency graph on cache invalidation, and nearly every model fails this contract. internal-suite, profile-artifacts, and selective-rebuild each block 8 of 12 models, while journal-query trips 7 of 12.

Performance gap

The round distribution spans 0–3/8, wider than it looks: DeepSeek-V4-Flash leads at 3/8 and 98% case pass, the only model to clear three rounds. The two 2/8 models diverge sharply — GPT-5.5 at 91% and DeepSeek-V4-Pro at 95% both reach round 2 with solid implementations, their losses concentrated in cache-manifest-mtime and content-hash-cache.

The large 1/8 cluster — Opus-4.8-xhigh (98%), Kimi-K2.6 (96%), Qwen3.6-Plus (96%), Qwen3.7-Max (80%), MiniMax-M3 (70%) — built nearly the full orchestrator but are pinned to one round by the same cache-propagation failures. At the bottom, GLM-5.1 (40%), GLM-5.2 (65%), and MiniMax-M2.7 (0%) complete zero rounds, with MiniMax-M2.7 passing no cases at all, indicating fundamental breakage well before the cache layer.
