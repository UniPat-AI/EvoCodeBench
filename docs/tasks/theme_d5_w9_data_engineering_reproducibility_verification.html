Difficulty analysis

This task builds dpipe, a deterministic Go data pipeline: ingest structured data, then validate, profile, transform (derive, fill, rollup, normalize, temporal, pivot…), compare, compute lineage, emit a reproducible manifest with a stable content hash, and audit/verify the run — 15 rounds, the longest chain in the suite, accumulating hundreds of cumulative cases. Determinism is the crux: the manifest must reproduce an exact checksum ("expected=a7b884ca… actual="), so any nondeterministic ordering or formatting fails the round.

The commonly-failed requirements span the whole pipeline — manifest, transform.fill, transform.derive, pipeline, transform.rename-drop and profile are each missed by 10–11 of the 12 models. Because the suite is cumulative over 15 rounds, a single early requirement a weak model never implements (e.g. profile or transform.derive) re-fails in every later round, which is why the weak side's per-requirement counts can run into the hundreds.

Performance gap

The headline story across 12 models is the chasm at the top: Opus-4.8 (10/15 rounds, 100% cases) stands alone, clearing two-thirds of the marathon while the entire rest of the field tops out at 2 rounds. Next are Qwen3.7-Max (2/15, 91% cases) and Qwen3.6-Plus (1/15, 96% cases) — Qwen3.6-Plus passes 96% of cases across all 15 rounds yet earns only 1 full round, the sharpest illustration of the binary reward masking near-complete work. GLM-5.1 and DeepSeek-V4-Pro each pass 1 round (80% and 45% cases respectively).

Below them case pass falls steeply and reflects real missing functionality: GPT-5.5 80%, Kimi-K2.6 74%, DeepSeek-V4-Flash 54%, MiniMax-M2.7 47%, MiniMax-M3 40%, Kimi-K2.7-Code 26%, and GLM-5.2 at 0% — producing no correct output at all. The dominant blockers for the non-Opus field are manifest, transform.fill, transform.derive, pipeline, transform.rename-drop, and profile, each unimplemented by 10–11 models and re-failing every downstream round — the same two-layered pattern: one model that mostly solves the determinism problem, and a wide field that never closes critical pipeline stages.
