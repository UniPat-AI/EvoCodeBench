Difficulty analysis

This task implements mlforge, a CLI for ML-checkpoint forensics: ingest checkpoints, extract metadata, reconstruct lineage and timeline, diff versions, query, and detect anomalies (gradient spikes, severity levels) — 9 rounds across 12 models. The work is mostly pure data transformation, so the chain rarely aborts mid-sequence; all 12 models run to round 9, making it a clean capability probe rather than an endurance test.

The load-bearing failures are layered. ingest (failed by 6 models, "got 5") cascades into timeline and lineage correctness, while detect-evidence and detect-csv (each failed by 7 models) expose gaps in anomaly-detection output formatting. query-not and query-paren (6 models each, "expected status ok, got got") reveal parser defects, and build failures (6 models) block entire rounds outright — so even high case-pass rates cannot translate into round completions.

Performance gap

This task discriminates sharply. Opus-4.8 and GLM-5.2 both pass all 9 rounds at 100% cases — the only models with full marks. GPT-5.5 is a close third at 7/9 rounds and 100% cases, losing only to a pair of late-round requirements. GLM-5.1 manages 1/9 rounds at 69% cases. Every other model lands at 0/9, despite case pass rates ranging from 35% (Kimi-K2.7-Code) up to 98% (Qwen3.6-Plus) and 92% (Kimi-K2.6).

The 0-round cluster illustrates how a high case score can mask a broken implementation: Qwen3.6-Plus at 98% cases and Kimi-K2.6 at 92% still fail every round because they cannot satisfy detect-evidence, detect-csv, or the query-not/query-paren parser requirements that gate full-round credit. Models at the lower end — MiniMax-M2.7 (72%), MiniMax-M3 (77%), Kimi-K2.7-Code (35%) — additionally stall on build and ingest failures. The decisive split is not breadth of effort but whether the model got the ingest contract, the anomaly-detection output schema, and the query parser all exactly right.

Is the task fair? (top-model check)

Claude Opus-4.8 — the strongest model in this evaluation — solved all 9 rounds at full marks, confirming the task is well-posed and fairly graded end-to-end; the reference (oracle) solution likewise passes every round. Weaker models' failures across ingest, detect-evidence, detect-csv, and the query-parser requirements therefore reflect genuine capability gaps, not ambiguous specifications or grading artifacts.
