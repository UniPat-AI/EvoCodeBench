Difficulty analysis

This is a brownfield migration: take the existing NumeriForge scientific-experiment platform, study its legacy API and reproducibility spec, and migrate it to deterministic, auditable execution — verify integrity, audit experiments, fingerprint environments, and compare with tolerances, across 8 rounds. Because it is reproducibility tooling, the determinism guarantees are the product, not a detail.

The defining requirement is seed-determinism (failed by 6 of 10 models): the same seed must produce a bit-identical result, e.g. "same seed produces identical residual" on an LU decomposition. The other shared miss is audit-single (5 models), the per-experiment audit whose corruption path must use exact "integrity check failed" terminology and surface env-mismatch warnings. Related determinism checks — ode-deterministic, checksum, tolerance-compare — pick off the weaker models.

Performance gap

The top tier is strong and well-separated. GPT-5.5 clears 7/8 rounds at 98%, losing only single cases of verify, audit-single and the verify-format/verbose variants. Kimi-K2.6 takes 5/8 (96%) on the same narrow misses scaled up, and Opus-4.8 lands 3/8 at 99% — its only weakness audit-batch (5 cases), an essentially complete tool that the round reward still penalizes heavily.

The other seven models all score 0/8, but the case column shows most of them are nearly there: GLM-5.1 (97%), MiniMax-M3 (96%), DeepSeek-V4-Pro (94%) and Qwen3.7-Max (94%) each lose almost exclusively to seed-determinism (16 cases) and a touch of ode-deterministic. That one determinism contract is the difference between their 0/8 and the leaders' high scores. Only the bottom pair genuinely lag — Qwen3.6-Plus at 70% (with seed-determinism blowing up to 88 cases) and MiniMax-M2.7 at 78% (adding tolerance-compare 32 and experiment-exec failures). The headline gap is not breadth of implementation but whether a model nailed deterministic seeding — exactly the thing the task exists to test.
