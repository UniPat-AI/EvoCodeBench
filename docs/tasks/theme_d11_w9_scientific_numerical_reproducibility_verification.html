Difficulty analysis

This is a brownfield migration: take the existing NumeriForge scientific-experiment platform, study its legacy API and reproducibility spec, and migrate it to deterministic, auditable execution — verify integrity, audit experiments, fingerprint environments, and compare with tolerances, across 8 rounds. Because it is reproducibility tooling, the determinism guarantees are the product, not a detail.

The two defining requirements — seed-determinism (failed by 8 of 12 models) and audit-batch (also 8 models) — form the load-bearing core: the same seed must produce a bit-identical estimate (e.g. "same seed produces identical pi_estimate"), and a batch audit of three seeded experiments must surface at least three AUDIT_PASS lines. Close behind are audit-single (7 models), which demands correct AUDIT_FAIL output on corrupted stored values, and verify (6 models), which must report VERIFIED for a valid result.

Performance gap

The top tier is narrow but real. MiniMax-M3 clears 7/8 rounds at 97%, the highest round count in the evaluation. GPT-5.5 follows at 6/8 (98% case), and Kimi-K2.6 takes 4/8 (96%) — all three consistently handle both seed-determinism and audit-batch where the field collapses. GLM-5.2 and Opus-4.8-xhigh each reach 2/8 at 94% and 91% respectively, suggesting partial determinism support that holds through early rounds only.

The remaining seven models score 0/8, yet their case percentages reveal how close many come: MiniMax-M2.7 (98%), GLM-5.1 (95%), and Kimi-K2.7-Code (76%) each pass the bulk of individual test cases but cannot sustain all four critical contracts across a full round. DeepSeek-V4-Pro (70%) and Qwen3.7-Max (80%) fall further, while DeepSeek-V4-Flash (33%) and Qwen3.6-Plus (82%) show the widest implementation gaps. The headline gap is not breadth of implementation but whether a model can nail both seeded determinism and batch auditing simultaneously — exactly what a reproducibility platform exists to guarantee.
