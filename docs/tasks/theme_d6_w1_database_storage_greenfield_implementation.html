Difficulty analysis

This is a from-scratch greenfield build of lsm-kvstore: a high-performance embedded key-value engine in Go using an LSM-Tree — write-ahead log, memtable, on-disk SSTables, compaction/merge, MVCC snapshots, transactions, TTL expiry and prefix compression, 8 rounds across 12 models. This is the deepest systems task in the set, and only one model passes even a single round: the round table is nearly all zeros.

Two failure modes dominate. First, many models score 0% on cases — MiniMax-M3, GLM-5.2, and MiniMax-M2.7 each finish at 0% case pass, while DeepSeek-V4-Pro (8%) and Qwen3.7-Max (22%) fare only marginally better. Second, even the higher-scoring models share precise behavioural gaps: the verify-command returns "ERROR: unknown command" because it was never implemented, and the entire merge operator feature — merge-compaction, merge-compaction-snapshot, merge-scan, merge-batch, and merge-persistence — returns NOT_FOUND instead of the merged value across 9 of 12 models.

Performance gap

With 11 of 12 models at 0/8 rounds, the only meaningful axis is case pass rate, which spreads from 92% down to 0%. Opus-4.8-xhigh leads at 92% cases, and GPT-5.5 is the sole model to clear a full round (1/8, 82% cases); these two are in a tier by themselves. Kimi-K2.6 (62%) and Qwen3.6-Plus (46%) form a mid-tier, followed by a cluster — Kimi-K2.7-Code (39%), GLM-5.1 (38%), and DeepSeek-V4-Flash (38%) — that reaches roughly a third of cases before stalling.

The floor is stark: MiniMax-M3, GLM-5.2, and MiniMax-M2.7 each score 0%, never producing a passing case, and DeepSeek-V4-Pro (8%) barely escapes that group. The shared wall across nearly all models is the merge operator: merge-compaction, merge-batch, merge-persistence, and merge-scan all return NOT_FOUND for 9 models, and verify-command likewise fails for 9 — together these requirements account for the ceiling that no model, not even the leaders, fully clears.
