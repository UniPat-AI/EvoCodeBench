Difficulty analysis

This is a brownfield scientific task: extend the existing numsim library with full sparse-matrix support, iterative Krylov solvers (CG, GMRES) with preconditioning, and a 2D Poisson solver that exercises all of it, across 7 rounds. The agent must respect numsim's existing matrix and history-output contracts rather than write fresh code.

Across 12 models the hardest commonly-failed requirements are poisson-mixed-bc (10 models) and history-format (8 models) — mixed-boundary Poisson and the exact tab-separated iteration log. poisson-nonsquare trips 7 models, while eig-shifted-inverse, history-precond-col and poisson-dirichlet each catch 6, confirming that Poisson boundary-condition handling and convergence-history formatting are the two structural barriers in this task.

Performance gap

The top tier is separated from the field by a sharp cliff. Opus-4.8 passes 7/7 rounds at 100%, and GPT-5.5 takes 6/7 at 99%, losing only narrow poisson-mixed-bc cases. DeepSeek-V4-Pro clears 2/7 at 89%, held back by history-format and Poisson boundary failures. Every other model finishes 0/7.

Among the zero-round group the case-pass spread is enormous — GLM-5.1 and DeepSeek-V4-Flash reach 96%, Qwen3.6-Plus 95%, and Kimi-K2.6 79%, all stalled specifically by poisson-mixed-bc and eig-shifted-inverse rather than structural collapse. By contrast Kimi-K2.7-Code (9%), MiniMax-M3 (13%), MiniMax-M2.7 (56%) and Qwen3.7-Max (60%) fail far earlier — their solvers crash outright, returning "exit code 1" on core requirements like poisson-dirichlet and history-precond-col. The separator is whether the iterative solver runs at all, not whether the reporting is pixel-perfect.

Is the task fair? (top-model check)

Claude Opus-4.8 solved all 7 rounds at full marks, and the oracle test suite passed every round as well, confirming that the task specification and grading are sound. Failures by weaker models on requirements such as poisson-mixed-bc and history-format therefore reflect genuine capability gaps — the ability to navigate numsim's existing contracts while correctly implementing mixed boundary conditions and exact convergence-log formatting — rather than any defect in the tests themselves.
