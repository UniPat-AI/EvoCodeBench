Difficulty analysis

This task builds repro-verify, a Go CLI that verifies the integrity and reproducibility of ML checkpoints and experiment artifacts: parse TOML manifests, audit lineage, compare outputs, detect drift, and emit reports across 8 rounds. All 10 models run the full chain (no aborts), so it reads as a clean capability probe — but the all-or-nothing round reward means a single missed assertion zeroes the round, and no model clears more than 3 of 8.

The load-bearing requirements, failed by most of the field, are report.lineage-detail (10/10 models — "assertion failed" on a report that should exit 0) and lineage.equal-timestamps (9/10), the lineage-reconstruction edge cases. Close behind are the TOML-parsing details toml.dotted-keys and toml.inline-comments (7 models each), report.comparison-missing (7), and drift.top-k (6) — the precise output-formatting and parsing contracts that separate a working tool from a correct one.

Performance gap

The leaders are Opus-4.8 and GPT-5.5, both 3/8 rounds at 100% and 95% case pass — near-complete tools that lose only the lineage-detail and equal-timestamp edge cases (GPT-5.5 additionally drops drift.threshold and drift.top-k, ~50 cases each, on the drift-analysis stage). DeepSeek-V4-Flash trails at 1/8 (94% cases), tripped mainly by TOML parsing — toml.inline-comments and json-errors.unreadable.

The remaining seven models all post 0/8 rounds, but the case-level view fractures them sharply. Qwen3.6-Plus passes 96% of cases — essentially a complete implementation losing only generate-report (32) and a few TOML cases — yet its round score is identical to GLM-5.1's, which collapses to 34% with generate-report (94), drift-analysis (72) and merge.first (64) all broken. Between those extremes sit MiniMax-M3 (82%), Qwen3.7-Max (71%), DeepSeek-V4-Pro (68%) and Kimi-K2.6 (50%), the latter two and Qwen3.7-Max sinking large case counts into compare.histogram (88–115 cases).
