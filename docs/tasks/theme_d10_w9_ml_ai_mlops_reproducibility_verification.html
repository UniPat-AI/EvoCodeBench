Difficulty analysis

This task builds repro-verify, a Go CLI that verifies the integrity and reproducibility of ML checkpoints and experiment artifacts: parse TOML manifests, audit lineage, compare outputs, detect drift, and emit reports across 8 rounds. All 12 models run the full chain (no aborts), so it reads as a clean capability probe — but the all-or-nothing round reward means a single missed assertion zeroes the round, and no model clears more than 2 of 8.

The load-bearing requirements, failed by most of the field, are report.lineage-detail (12/12 models — "assertion failed" on a report that should exit 0) and lineage.equal-timestamps (12/12), the lineage-reconstruction edge cases. Close behind are report.comparison-missing (9 models), generate-report and toml.inline-comments (8 models each), and audit-lineage.unresolved-parent (7 models) — the precise output-formatting and parsing contracts that separate a working tool from a correct one.

Performance gap

The sole round leader is DeepSeek-V4-Pro at 2/8 rounds (98% case pass), clearing more of the lineage and comparison stages than anyone else. Opus-4.8 and GPT-5.5 each reach 1/8 rounds at 98% and 99% case pass respectively — near-complete tools that still stumble on the universal blockers report.lineage-detail and lineage.equal-timestamps.

The remaining nine models all post 0/8 rounds, but the case-level view fractures them sharply. MiniMax-M3 leads the zero-round group at 93%, followed by DeepSeek-V4-Flash (92%) and Qwen3.6-Plus (90%) — essentially complete implementations derailed only by the strictest assertion checks. At the other extreme, Kimi-K2.7-Code collapses to 0% and GLM-5.2 to 30%, with GLM-5.1 (40%) and Qwen3.7-Max (57%) also struggling broadly. The round column flattens a 0%-to-99% capability range into a wall of zeros for all but one model.

Is the task fair? (top-model check)

Opus-4.8 cleared round 1 at full marks then stalled at round 2 ("The TOML parsing is fragile"), passing 145/151 cases; the 6 failing cases all belong to well-specified error-handling contracts: json-errors.usage (expected exit 1), json-errors.toplevel (expected structured error JSON on stdout), and json-errors.unreadable (expected exit 1) — concrete, unambiguous assertions that the oracle satisfies every round. These are genuine implementation gaps by a top-tier model, not grading artifacts, confirming that the harder rounds reflect real capability headroom rather than any defect in the task or test suite.
