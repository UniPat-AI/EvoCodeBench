Difficulty analysis

This is a long migration task on mlpipe, a Go CLI for ML inference-pipeline management: a model registry, checkpoint loading, comparison, pruning, ensembles, import/export and weight-format upgrades — 13 rounds across 12 models. The unusual depth means a model has many chances to accumulate features but also many places for one broken subcommand to cascade.

The commonly-failed requirements are mostly whole subcommands that exit non-zero or produce wrong output: missing-dir (11 models — "not found"), compare (9 — "exit=1 expected=0"), upgrade (8), stats (8 — "correlation"), diff (8 — "mismatch") and import (7 — "exit=1 expected=0"). Nearly every model fails directory setup, making correct round completion rare even for high case-pass models.

Performance gap

The top tier separates sharply from the field. Opus-4.8 clears 12/13 rounds at 99% case pass, the strongest showing in this evaluation. GLM-5.2 (99%, 9/13) is the only other model to pass multiple rounds, with both achieving near-perfect case rates — the gap between them and the rest is stark. Kimi-K2.6 (91%, 4/13) follows at a distance, clearing 4 rounds but stalling on compare and upgrade.

The bulk of the field sits at 0–1 rounds despite meaningful case-pass rates: MiniMax-M3 (92%, 1/13), Qwen3.6-Plus (90%, 1/13) and DeepSeek-V4-Flash (90%, 1/13) all exceed 90% cases yet land at 1 round, held back by missing-dir, diff and stats. At the bottom, GPT-5.5 (56%) and Kimi-K2.7-Code (61%) fail broadly across subcommands, while DeepSeek-V4-Pro (72%) and Qwen3.7-Max (74%) lose ground on upgrade and compare. The 90%-case / 0-or-1-round cluster is the defining feature of this task: case reward dramatically overstates functional completeness.
